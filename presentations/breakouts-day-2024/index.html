A more adaptive web

Who needs a more adaptive web?

  • People with cognitive and learning disabilities (e.g. ADHD, short-term memory loss)
  • People who are in a time of crisis; have a migraine; are busy
  • People who use Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)
  • People who struggle to learn/recognise new icons

All of us, at some time or another!

What support could look like

WAI-Adapt's proposed approach

Build on existing infrastructure

  • The Cognitive Accessibility Task Force identified a series of common destinations.

    The challenge was how to expose them…

  • Through APA WG's accessibility review work, we discovered A Well-Known URL for Changing Passwords.

  • This builds on well-known URIs, providing a standard way to expose sites' common destinations.

Explainer and draft specification

  • We wrote an "Explainer" of our proposed approach, for review..

  • We also have an early draft of the specification.

Next steps

Next steps

  • Seek review from other W3C groups—on the proposed approach; and the destinations to be standardised.

  • Establish our basic set of features; refine their design.

  • Seek implementations (sites that follow the spec; assistive technologies that present the results to users).
